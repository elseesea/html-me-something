Avoiding Phishing

Cyber criminals steal information and money at an alarming frequency. Most often, the first step in their process is to get a user to fall for a phish.

A phish is a communication designed to get the user to take some action that would enable the cyber criminal to move to the next step in the process.

The most common type of phish is sent via email. And usually, the email asks the user to click a link or open an attachment

But, oftentimes, there are red flags in a malicious email. Read on to learn about some red flags.

From

  1. The sender's email address is not one I normally communicate with.
  2. The email is from someone not in my company & it's unrelated to my responsibilities.
  3. The email is from someone in my company, but is very unusual or out of character.

To

  1. The email was also sent to a unexpected combination of people.

Hyperlinks

  1. Hovering over the hyperlink with the mouse shows that the link-to address is for an unexpected website.
  2. Hovering over the hyperlink with the mouse shows that the link-to address in for a different website.

Language

  1. Unusual language, misspellings, bad grammar.
  2. For example, "Kindly review ..."

Urgent Action

  1. If you're asked to take action urgently, or given a short deadline, it's a red flag.

Remember: Only you can prevent a phishing attack!
